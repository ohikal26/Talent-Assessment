How this works

You score each person twice, from 1 to 10. The first score is performance against their KPIs. The second is how well they live the core values. The horizontal axis is performance, the vertical axis is values, and the lines cross at the middle of each scale.

That gives four quadrants, and the top right holds a smaller square in the corner. A players sit inside it, scoring 7.5 or higher on both performance and values. Since the scale is whole numbers, that means an 8 or above on each. Potential A players fill the rest of the top right: high on both, but not yet over the bar. B players sit top left, living the values with performance still to build. C players sit bottom left, low on both. Toxic performers sit bottom right, delivering results while working against the values. The toxic quadrant is the one most leaders avoid, and the one that matters most, because keeping a high performer who breaks the values tells everyone else the values are optional.
