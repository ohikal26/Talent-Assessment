The Talent Matrix

Performance × Values · Five or Six at a Time

Two things tell you whether someone belongs on the team: whether they deliver, and whether they live the values. This plots both, so you can see your team for what it is and decide who to grow, who to coach, and who to act on.

Name your core values, enter the initials of your five or six direct reports, and score each person on performance and on every value. Nothing here is saved or sent anywhere. It stays in your browser, so you can be honest.

How this works

You score each person on two things, from 1 to 10. The first is performance against their KPIs. The second is values, and that score is the average of how they live each of your core values, weighted equally. Score someone high on most values but low on one, and that one pulls their average down. That is the point: a person does not get to live some values and ignore others.

The horizontal axis is performance, the vertical axis is the values average, and the lines cross at the middle of each scale. That gives four quadrants, and the top right holds a smaller square in the corner. A players sit inside it, at 7.5 or higher on both performance and the values average. Potential A players fill the rest of the top right: high on both, but not yet over the bar. B players sit top left, living the values with performance still to build. C players sit bottom left, low on both. Toxic performers sit bottom right, delivering results while working against the values.

A person only appears on the matrix once you have scored their performance and every core value, so the average is always complete.
